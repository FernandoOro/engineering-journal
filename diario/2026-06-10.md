# 2026-06-10 (Wednesday)

**Context:** Backend on a fintech SaaS platform (client) — reducing attack surface.

## What I did

### 1. Remove a never-shipped feature module
- **Diagnosis:** the feature never reached production — no stored data, no frontend
  navigation, and no service consuming it.
- **Implementation:** deleted the router, its docs, and the associated frontend
  components, then removed **every** reference along the chain: app wiring, route
  optimizer, Postgres stores/repositories, payload normalizers, and the data-purge
  blocks. Verified the core engine was untouched.
- **Scope:** ~660 lines removed across 9 files.

### 2. Remove a dead content-library router
- **Diagnosis:** the router was never called in production. To confirm, I added
  instrumentation logs and observed **zero hits**; the real content persists through a
  different path.
- **Implementation:** unwired and deleted the router (~270 lines).

## Ops
- Verified the app booted without the removed routers and that no imports dangled
  (searched for orphan references before pushing); smoke-tested the live engine endpoints.

## Learnings / technical decisions
- **Measure before deleting:** instrumenting with logs and confirming zero traffic turns
  "I think this is unused" into evidence. I deleted with confidence.
- Dead code isn't neutral: every exposed router is attack surface and every legacy module
  is debt. Removing ~900 lines is security work, not just cleanup.

## What I could do better
- One module had references across 9 files in different layers (routes, stores, repos,
  services, purge). A dependency map up front would have made the removal more mechanical
  and saved the back-and-forth of hunting the next reference.
